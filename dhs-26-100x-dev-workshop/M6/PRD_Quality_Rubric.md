# ✅ PRD Quality Rubric
### Score a neighbour's PRD · M6 peer-review · the 100x Masterclass

> AI drafts a PRD in seconds. **Your judgment** is what makes it good. Score each dimension **1–5** and give **one concrete suggestion**. A great PRD is short, clear, and *decidable*.

---

## Score each 1–5

| # | Dimension | 1 (weak) | 5 (strong) | Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Problem & user** | Vague "make X better" | Specific problem + who has it | ☐ |
| 2 | **Goals** | A feature list | Outcomes the product should achieve | ☐ |
| 3 | **Scope boundary** | Only says what's in | States what's **in and out** (non-goals) | ☐ |
| 4 | **Requirements** | Unclear / untestable | Each has a **testable acceptance criterion** | ☐ |
| 5 | **Success metrics** | "Users like it" | **Measurable** (number + timeframe) | ☐ |
| 6 | **Grounded** | Imagined / generic | Matches the **real app** | ☐ |
| 7 | **Concise** | Rambles, multi-page | **One page**, no fluff | ☐ |

## Quick read
- **30–35** — ship it; a stakeholder could decide from this.
- **22–29** — solid; tighten the weakest one or two dimensions.
- **< 22** — regenerate with sharper inputs (name the user, add non-goals, make metrics measurable).

## The three questions that catch most problems
1. **Could an engineer build the wrong thing and still satisfy this?** → requirements aren't tight enough.
2. **Could we tell in a month whether it worked?** → success isn't measurable.
3. **Does it say what we're *not* doing?** → scope isn't bounded.
