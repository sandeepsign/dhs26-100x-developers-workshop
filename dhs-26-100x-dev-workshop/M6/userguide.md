# TaskFlow — User Guide
*Reference example · plain-language, for people who will actually use the app*

Welcome to **TaskFlow** — a simple shared board that shows what your team is working on. No training needed. Here's everything you can do.

## Open TaskFlow
Open the link your team shared (it looks like `https://taskflow-….replit.app`). It works in any browser, and on your phone via the TaskFlow app. There's nothing to install to use the web version.

## The board at a glance
Your team's work shows up in three columns:

- **To-Do** — not started yet
- **In Progress** — being worked on now
- **Done** — finished

Each **card** is one task and shows its **title**, who it's **assigned to**, and its **due date**.

## Create a task
1. Click **+ Add task**.
2. Type a **title** (this is the only required field).
3. Optionally pick an **assignee** and a **due date**.
4. Save — your task appears in **To-Do** right away.

> Tip: keep titles short and action-oriented, e.g. *"Send the July invoice."*

## Move a task
As work progresses, move a card from **To-Do → In Progress → Done**. Your change is saved automatically and everyone sees the same board.

## Find what you need — filter
Use the **filter** to focus:

- **By assignee** — see just your tasks (or a teammate's).
- **By status** — see everything that's *In Progress*, for example.

Clear the filter to see the whole board again.

## Frequently asked

**Do I need an account?** No — anyone with the link can view and use the board.

**Where does my data go?** Tasks are saved to the app's database and stay there between visits.

**It's slow to load the first time.** On the free hosting, the app "sleeps" after a few minutes idle and takes a few seconds to wake — that's normal.

**Can I use it on my phone?** Yes — open the link in your mobile browser, or use the TaskFlow app (Expo).
